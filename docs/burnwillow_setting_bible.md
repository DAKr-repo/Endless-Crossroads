# Burnwillow Setting Bible
**Version 2.0 | Game Design Reference**
**Genre:** Tabletop Roguelite / Dungeon Crawler
**Core Hook:** You are what you wear.
**Tone:** High fantasy, druidic, shamanic — not biopunk. The Arborists sang wood into shape. Amber is sacred, not industrial.

---

## Overview

The Burnwillow universe is a single living cosmos contained within four world-trees called the Groves. Each tree is a complete civilization, a biome, and a metaphysical principle. The canopy is the sky. The roots are the underworld. There is nothing else. The trees are dying. The Rot is winning. The players are the last ones trying to stop it.

---

## The Four Groves

The Groves are four world-trees of planetary scale, each embodying a season and a philosophical state. These are not metaphors — the seasons are real, structural properties of each tree's biology and the civilizations it supports. The canopy of each Grove is its entire sky. The Deep Shade beyond the reach of each canopy's fire-light is a frozen, pitch-black expanse where no life can survive.

### Burnwillow — Autumn (Active Game Zone)
- The dying hearth. Fire-leaves in perpetual crimson, orange, and gold — the only source of warmth and light in existence.
- Amber sap still flows in the upper veins, but the deep roots are compromised.
- Emberhome clings to the mid-trunk — a village of scavengers and survivors.
- The Blight rose first here. The Burnwillow is the front line.
- The tree physically thrashes in pain from the Rot — crushing old tunnels, tearing open new ones. This is why dungeons are procedural: geography is genuinely unstable.
- Atmosphere: woodsmoke, petrified bark corridors, amber-lit chambers, silence broken by distant fungal growth sounds.
- **Theme:** Harvest and decay. What you gather from the dying tree to survive another day. Scarcity, sacrifice, the warmth of community against encroaching darkness.

### Verdhollow — Spring (Drowning in Growth)
- Uncontrolled growth. Everything blooms too fast, too wild. Roots split stone overnight, vines strangle paths between visits, new life smothers old.
- The Blight here wears green — indistinguishable from natural growth until it is too late.
- The druids of Verdhollow are not tending a dying tree — they are desperately pruning one that won't stop growing.
- Root-Roads from Verdhollow are choked and overgrown; passage is treacherous without Arborist clearance tools.
- **Theme:** Abundance as threat. The terror of plenty. Life without restraint. The pruning knife as the most sacred tool.

### Solheart — Summer (The Scorched Throne)
- A tree burning from the outside in. Crown fires. Drought in the roots.
- Its people are desert-pragmatists: nothing wasted, nothing sentimental.
- Liquid Aether here runs hot and unstable — raw aether burns rather than illuminates.
- Amber from Solheart cracks under pressure; its Memory Seeds contain only heat-scorched fragments.
- **Theme:** Excess and endurance. The forge-heat of peak power. Glory and its cost. Strength that consumes itself.

### Ashenmere — Winter (The Still Place)
- A tree that has stopped entirely. No Root-Song. No sap flow. No apparent Blight.
- Crystalline, ice-encased. The sap is frozen in the veins like trapped amber.
- Whether Ashenmere is dead, dormant, or waiting is unknown.
- Its silence is considered a bad omen by all Grove cultures.
- The only Grove where Hollows stand motionless rather than advancing.
- **Theme:** Stillness and forgetting. Preservation vs. stagnation. The beauty of death. What endures when everything else stops.

### The Root-Roads
- Ancient sealed passages deep beneath each Grove, connecting all four world-trees.
- Built and maintained by the Arborists. The First Druids walked these roads freely to tend the seasonal cycle.
- Sealed since the Golden Age collapse — forgotten, overgrown, warded.
- Each passage carries the character of the season it leads to: a road to Verdhollow drips with sap and sprouts vines; a road to Ashenmere grows cold and silent with frost.
- Navigating Root-Roads requires a Dowsing Rod or successful Wits check (DC 16 Heroic).
- The Blight travels Root-Roads. Opening a sealed passage is never without consequence.
- Where the Rot has spread, Root-Roads become Hollow highways — infested and impassable.
- **Campaign hook:** The Root-Roads are the multiverse spine. Each Grove is its own full campaign setting, connected by these deep passages.

## The Rot (The Blight)

The Rot is not a creature, disease, or army. It is a process — entropy given biological form.

### Nature
- Parasitic fungal corruption rising from the deepest root layers of all Groves.
- Cold, grey, and utterly silent. It does not hunger or hate. It simply converts.
- Spreads through moisture, darkness, and stillness. Fire and motion slow it; nothing stops it permanently.
- Eats living things from the inside out. Victims remain ambulatory and appear functional until collapse.
- The Rot-Heart (deep Zone 4) is where the infection originates in the Burnwillow.

### Hollows
- Creatures and people consumed by the Blight become Hollows: mindless husks.
- Hollows retain physical capability and muscle memory (mechanically: Hollowed Scavengers use equipped gear traits with full proficiency).
- They do not speak, sleep, or retreat. They advance.
- Fallen Seekers become Hollows. They may one day shamble back toward the very village they called home.
- Hollows of large creatures (bears, serpents) retain their size and special attacks intact.

### The Root-Song and Rot Silence
- The Burnwillow communicates through a subsonic vibration called the Root-Song — felt, not heard.
- Sensitive individuals (Aether 14+) can interpret fragments: warnings, memories, emotional states.
- Where the Rot spreads, the Root-Song falls silent. This silence is the clearest danger indicator in the dungeon.
- Zone-depth attenuation: Zone 1-2 has strong Root-Song; Zone 3 receives partial impressions; Zone 4 is acoustically dead.
- Emberhome is alive with Root-Song. Amber Vaults are acoustically sealed inside — their silence is clean, not Rot-silence.

### The Blight's Vector
- The Rot does not strategize. Its vector is the Burnwillow's central sap-fire core.
- If the fire is extinguished, the Burnwillow dies. The Root-Roads open. Every Grove falls.

## Aether, Amber, and the Shamanic Economy

### Liquid Aether — The Lifeblood
- The golden sap of the Burnwillow. The tree's circulatory fluid and magical substrate.
- Powers all Arborist technology. Flows through vein-channels carved into the trunk walls.
- Can be harvested with appropriate tools (costs 1 Doom Clock advance per extraction).
- Raw Aether is mildly toxic to non-attuned individuals (DC 10 Grit or -1 to all rolls for 1 hour).
- Aether runs are the primary power source for Emberhome's light, heat, and forge-fire.

### Amber — Vessels of Memory
- When Aether hardens around a moment of significance — a death, a decision, a song — it becomes Amber.
- Every shard holds a memory. Attuned characters (Aether 14+) can sense impressions from amber fragments.
- Trading amber is not an economic transaction. It is the passing of memory. Spending amber means releasing a piece of the tree's history. Crafting with amber means reshaping memory into form.
- The most precious amber contains the oldest memories — from before the Rot, from the age of the First Druids.
- Breaking amber unnecessarily is a grave social transgression in every Grove culture.
- **Sun-Shards:** Fragments of the tree's original amber heart. The most potent amber in existence.

### Amber Functions (Mechanical)
- **Currency:** Standard unit of trade in Emberhome and all Grove settlements.
- **Crafting Material:** Required for Tier III+ item construction.
- **Sacred Relic:** Cultural and spiritual touchstone.
- **Power Source:** High-density chunks can power Arborist constructs and implements.
- **Lore Delivery:** Specific amber pieces carry readable memories — the primary lore drop mechanism.

### Amber Quality Grades
| Grade | Formation Time | Memory Strength | Mechanical Value |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Dust | Hours | None | Trace crafting filler |
| Chip | Days | Impressionistic | Minor component |
| Shard | Months | Clear scene | Standard currency unit |
| Core | Years | Full memory | Tier III crafting material |
| Heart | Decades | Living presence | Tier IV component, relic quality |

## Sun-Fruits

- Grow exclusively in the Crown — the highest, still-burning branches of the Burnwillow.
- Carry condensed tree-fire: bioluminescent, warm to the touch, smell of ember and resin.
- Each fruit is unique; no two have identical potency.

### Mechanical Uses
- **Rot Cure:** Consuming a Sun-Fruit halts active Blight infection and reverses early-stage corruption.
- **HP Restoration:** Heals 2d6+2 HP (bypasses DR — internal healing).
- **Aether Recharge:** Restores 1 charge to a depleted Implement.
- **Doom Reduction:** Reduces Doom Clock by 1 point (costly — represents genuine relief from pressure).

### Why Harvesting is Lethal
- The Crown is the most dangerous zone in the Burnwillow.
- Apex predators — Canopy Wardens, Emberstorm Phoenixes — treat the Crown as territory.
- Wind currents at Crown altitude turn ranged combat into chaos (all ranged attacks: -1d6).
- Descent with fruits is the real challenge: fruits degrade if exposed to Rot-tainted air below Zone 3.

## Arborist Technology — The Lost Art

The Arborists were the original civilization of the Groves. They did not forge metal — they sang wood into shapes harder than steel and wove Aether into silk. This was not technology. It was communion. The Arborist spoke to the tree and the tree answered. To find an Arborist relic is to hear an echo of the old songs.

### The Arborists
- Ancient order of tree-shapers and biomancers who tended all four Groves during the Golden Age.
- Organized as a guild-monastic order: part scientists, part priests of the tree.
- Created the Amber Vault network, Wood-Golem Constructs, Ironbark technology, and the Root-Road infrastructure.
- Disappeared during the Golden Age collapse — the precise cause is unknown. Leading theories: internal schism, early Blight contact, or deliberate withdrawal into the Root-Roads.
- Their relics are the most mechanically powerful items in the game (Tier IV+).

### Arborist Relic Properties
- Items carry the `[Resonant]` trait: they hum audibly near active Root-Song.
- Most require Aether 12+ to attune; unintuned use grants only Tier I benefits regardless of material.
- Cannot be repaired conventionally — only Arborist forges (found in Amber Vaults) can restore them.
- Some retain partial sentience; the Sun-Bow selects its own wielder (Aether check DC 16 to attune).
- Arborist items are grown, not made. They respond to the Root-Song, not to the wielder's will alone.

### Known Arborist Constructs and Items
- **Wood-Golem Constructs:** Autonomous tree-limb guardians. Found exclusively in Amber Vaults. Guard against the Rot, not people. Will attack characters who carry Blight-tainted items. Cannot leave Vault interiors.
- **Ironbark Plating:** Bark treated with Aether singing to exceed steel hardness. DR 3 as chest armor. Cannot be cold-forged — requires Arborist forge or Memory Seed recipe.
- **Dowsing Rod:** Tool item. Hums when within 2 rooms of a Secret or hidden Amber Vault. Does not consume a Doom advance.
- **Amber Vein Taps:** Fixed installations in dungeon walls. Provide 1 Aether charge per use. Single-use per run.
- **Sun-Bow:** Tier IV ranged weapon (Crown-tier access required). Selects its own wielder.
- **Whisper-Key:** Unlocks any Amber Vault when held to the shell. Consumed on use.

## Amber Vaults

Amber Vaults are the Arborists' legacy infrastructure — sealed emergency caches created at the moment of the Golden Age collapse, preserved untouched ever since.

### Physical Description
- Indestructible Iron-Amber spheres, 10-30 meters diameter, fused into the Burnwillow's trunk and root walls.
- Interior atmosphere is sealed and clean — no Rot, no humidity, no decay.
- Lit by bioluminescent Aether veins running through the walls. Constant faint hum. Root-Song acoustically isolated.
- Guarded by Wood-Golem Constructs.

### Metroidvania Progression
- Vaults are visible early in runs — the amber shell glows faintly through dungeon walls.
- Cannot be opened by brute force. The shell is genuinely indestructible to any Tier I-II tool.
- **Access Methods:**
  - Whisper-Key (Tier III item, found in earlier Vaults or as rare dungeon loot)
  - Aether Resonance (DC 20 Legendary Aether check)
  - Specific dungeon puzzle solutions
- The canonical "Teased Vault" from Module 0: the Sun-Bow behind unbreakable amber. Players see it. They cannot have it. They come back.
- Vaults do not appear on any dungeon map. Must be located via Dowsing Rod or successful Wits check (DC 12 Hard).

### Vault Loot Profile
- Guaranteed Tier III+ loot (at least one build-defining item per Vault).
- Signature items: Sun-Bow, Whisper-Key, Arborist's Mantle, Resonance Band.
- 50% chance to contain a Memory Seed.

### Wood-Golem Guard Protocol
- Non-Blighted intruders: Golems observe. They do not initiate combat.
- First item taken freely. Second item triggers guardian protocol unless disabled (Aether check DC 14: communicate intent to the golem).
- Blighted intruders: Immediate hostile response.

## Memory Seeds

Memory Seeds are the core roguelite meta-progression mechanic. They convert individual run discoveries into persistent campaign knowledge.

### Physical Description
- Fossilized nuts or amber-encased seed-pods, roughly fist-sized.
- Carry compressed Arborist crafting blueprints — not instructions but living memory-impressions.
- Glow with soft Aether light that cannot be dimmed or concealed.

### Mechanical Function
- Found in Amber Vaults: 50% chance per Vault.
- Must be physically carried from discovery point to Emberhome to unlock the recipe.
- Upon delivery, Emberhome's forge masters extract and decode the blueprint.
- Unlocks a crafting recipe permanently within the **current campaign save file**.

### Per-Save Scoping (Critical Design Rule)
- Unlocks are scoped to the campaign save, not globally.
- A solo campaign's unlocked recipes do not carry into a separate 4-player campaign.
- Rationale: each campaign represents a distinct group of survivors on a distinct run of history.
- Consequence: a 4-player campaign must find and extract Seeds that the solo player already found. No shared inheritance.

### The Extraction Penalty
Carrying a Memory Seed imposes severe tactical costs — designed to force escort-extraction scenarios.

| Penalty | Mechanical Effect |
|---------|------------------|
| Inventory Slot | Occupies 1 precious backpack slot for the carrier |
| Aether Beacon | Glow cannot be concealed. Stealth impossible. Adjacent-room enemies alerted. |
| Speed Cap | Carrier cannot use the Dash action. Movement limited to standard 30ft. |
| Doom Acceleration | Doom Clock advances at 2x rate while Seed is in inventory |
| Tactical Result | Carrier must be protected. Every combat becomes a bodyguard scenario. |

## Emberhome — The Last Bastion

The last permanent settlement in the Burnwillow. Built directly into the petrified, stone-like bark of the Burnwillow's massive lower roots.

### Structure
- Population: approximately 200 survivors, scavengers, and specialists.
- Two vocations: **Seekers** (those who delve) and **Kindlers** (those who tend the village and Hearth).
- Power source: active Aether vein running beneath the settlement. The sap-fire hearth has burned for 90 years without interruption.
- Exterior walls: Ironbark planks and resin-sealed bark. Holds against Tier I-II Hollow incursions.
- The settlement shakes periodically as the Burnwillow thrashes. Everyone has learned to sleep through it.

### Key Locations

**The Hearth**
A massive hollowed-out burl warmed by a Sun-Shard. The spiritual and physical center of Emberhome. Tended by Elder Kaelen. The Root-Song is strongest here — audible as a faint vibration in the walls on quiet nights.

**The Amber-Smithy (Ember-Forge)**
A humid, greenhouse-like crafting chamber. Primary forge masters: Weld and Kettrick. Memory Seeds are brought here for decoding. Arborist-adjacent in approach — wood-grown objects, not metal-forged.

**The Root-Cellar**
A cramped, cluttered shop run by a paranoid scavenger named Twitch. Filled with Pre-Rot junk — some worthless, some priceless. Twitch doesn't always know which is which.

### Hub Functions (Between Runs)
- **Rest:** Party recovers HP. Doom Clock does not advance in Emberhome.
- **Craft:** Ember-Forge builds from unlocked Memory Seed recipes. Requires Amber as material cost.
- **Trade:** Merchant (rotating, NPC: Birsa) carries Tier I-II consumables, keys, occasional Tier II gear.
- **Upgrade:** Specific Emberhome structures can be improved with Amber across runs (campaign-persistent).
- **Lore:** NPCs accumulate memory of party actions. Elder Kaelen holds oldest institutional knowledge.

## The Doom Clock

The Doom Clock tracks the party's cumulative impact on the dungeon's ecosystem. It is not a timer — it is a pressure system. Every action has consequence.

### Mechanics
- Starts at 0 each run.
- Decreases only by consuming a Sun-Fruit (reduces by 1).
- Advances by 1 per room transition.
- Advances by 2 while a Memory Seed is in the party's inventory.
- Additional advances triggered by: combat noise, failed stealth checks, sprung traps, extended Aether extractions.

### Threshold Events (Canonical Engine Values)
| Threshold | Event |
|-----------|-------|
| 5 | "The torches flicker. Distant growls echo." Enemy patrol routes shift. Adjacent-room monsters become alert. |
| 10 | "Something stirs at the dungeon entrance. The hunt begins." Rot Hunter spawns at entry room. |
| 13 | "Patrol routes shift. Enemies are alert." All enemy DC checks increase by +2. |
| 17 | "The Blight spreads. HP cannot be restored naturally." All healing items and natural rest are inactive. |
| 22 | "The dungeon seals. No escape." Root-Road exits lock. Must fight to the zone boss or die. |

### The Rot Hunter
- Spawns at Doom 10 at the dungeon entry room.
- Apex Hollow — the largest, most corrupted creature type available in the current zone.
- BFS pathfinding: always moves toward the party's last known position, never loses the trail.
- Cannot be defeated with Tier I-II weapons (DR 3, boss-tier HP pool).
- Forces combat if it enters the party's current room. Intended response: retreat.
- Drops an artifact-quality item if defeated — the only Tier IV loot path outside Amber Vaults.

## Dungeon Zone Structure

The Burnwillow is divided into vertical zones from the roots upward. Because the tree thrashes from Rot damage, dungeon geometry is procedurally generated each run.

| Zone | Name | Depth | Enemy Tiers | Boss | Notes |
|------|------|-------|-------------|------|-------|
| 0 | Emberhome Approaches | Surface | I | Tutorial only | Adjacent to Emberhome; safe corridors |
| 1 | The Tangle | Root Level | I-II | Hollowed Bear | Damp, cramped, moss and glowing mushrooms. Most explored. |
| 2 | The Stone-Grip | Deep Earth | II-III | The Rust King | Cold, ancient. Cracked stone. Arborist settlement ruins. |
| 3 | The Aether-Flow | Mid-Root | III-IV | The Blight Mother | Rivers of raw golden sap. Exposure grants visions or burns minds. |
| 4 | The Rot-Heart | Core | IV | The Clockwork Archon | Grey ash. Root-Song absent. Pulsating fungal webs. Infection origin. |
| 5 | The Crown | Canopy | IV + Crown | The Void Herald | Vertigo, high winds, blinding fire-light. Sun-Fruit harvest zone. |

**The Veins:** The tree's internal vascular system. Narrow, turbulent chutes pumping sap between zones. Can be hacked into as rapid shortcuts between zones (costly: HP or Keys to open each Vein access point).

## Key NPCs

### Elder Kaelen (Emberhome)
- **Function:** Quest giver, institutional knowledge, moral anchor of the settlement.
- **Quirk:** Runs his thumb along an old burn scar on his left forearm when making difficult decisions.
- **Secret:** Opened a sealed Root-Road passage 20 years ago out of desperation. Three Seekers died. He told no one.
- **History:** Survivor of the first major Rot incursion into Emberhome. Organized the evacuation. Lost his family in it.
- **Relationships:** Trusted by Weld, unknown to Kettrick (who has his own secret), deferred to by most Kindlers.
- **Want vs Need:** Says he wants the Burnwillow saved. Needs to believe the deaths he caused were not wasted.
- **Voice:** Precise, unhurried. Never uses contractions when discussing the Blight. "It does not stop. We do."

### Weld (Ember-Forge Master)
- **Function:** Primary crafter, Memory Seed decoder, equipment upgrader.
- **Quirk:** Talks to items while working on them. Not for luck — he claims they answer back.
- **Secret:** Has been using communal Aether from the settlement vein for personal experiments. Emberhome's reserves are 15% lower than official records show.
- **History:** Apprenticed under an Arborist-trained smith in a now-destroyed settlement. The only survivor of that community.
- **Relationships:** Respects Elder Kaelen. Competitive with Kettrick. Owes a debt to merchant Birsa (she brought the tools he needs and never named her price).
- **Want vs Need:** Wants to be recognized as the equal of Arborist craftsmen. Needs to accept that keeping people alive with imperfect tools is the same thing.
- **Voice:** Tactile vocabulary. "This joint's wrong." "Feel that? That's the wood arguing with itself."

### Kettrick (Ember-Forge Second)
- **Function:** Alternative crafter, gossip hub, faction liaison.
- **Quirk:** Keeps a small piece of Ashenmere bark in his pocket. Claims it helps him think. It has never been warm.
- **Secret:** In contact via Root-Road messenger with a Verdhollow settlement Emberhome believes was destroyed.
- **Relationships:** Rivals Weld. Close with several dungeon delvers who trade him advance scouting intel.
- **Want vs Need:** Wants out — to make it to another Grove, any Grove. Needs to understand there is no safer place.
- **Voice:** Casual, conspiratorial. Treats every conversation like a trade.

### Twitch (Root-Cellar)
- **Function:** Merchant of Pre-Rot salvage, incidental lore drops via junk descriptions.
- **Quirk:** Catalogues items by the emotion they give him, not their function. "This one feels like early frost. That one feels like waiting."
- **Secret:** Has found a functional Arborist communication crystal in his junk pile. Does not know what it is. It occasionally hums.
- **Voice:** Rapid, associative, prone to trailing off. Finishes sentences with questions.

### Birsa (Rotating Merchant)
- **Function:** Trade, rumors, faction intelligence.
- **Quirk:** Never names a price twice the same way. Always frames value relationally: "Worth more than that lockpick. Less than your left hand."
- **Secret:** Has a Hollow-detection sense — can smell early-stage Blight infection. Has identified one early-stage infected in Emberhome and said nothing.
- **Voice:** Oblique. Never answers a direct question directly.

### The Burnwillow (The Tree Itself)
- Semi-sentient. Not sapient in the conventional sense — no language, no agenda.
- Communicates through Root-Song: intensifying vibration near danger, harmonic resonance near Amber Vaults, dissonance near Rot concentrations.
- Characters with Aether 16+ receive clear emotional impressions: grief, urgency, recognition.
- The tree recognizes parties that have returned repeatedly. Its Root-Song changes around proven Seekers.

## The Lock-and-Key Economy

Resource management is a primary tension layer, separate from combat.

- **Keys:** Consumable. Auto-open 1 Locked Chest or Door. Drop from Hollowed Scavengers, sold at Emberhome.
- **Lockpicking:** Requires `[Lockpick]` trait gear (e.g., Burglar's Gloves). Roll Wits vs. Lock DC. Failure: lock jams or trap triggers (advances Doom by 1).
- **Brute Force (Might):** Opens anything. 50% chance to destroy fragile loot inside. Loud — advances Doom by 1.
- **Magic (Aether):** Clean, silent open. Consumes 1 Implement charge.

---

## Magic System Summary

- **Implements (Wands/Staves):** Reusable. Scale with Aether stat. Hit Resistance (not Defense) — bypasses armor class entirely.
- **Scrolls:** Consumable, high power. Roll Aether vs. DC 10 on use. Failure burns the scroll with no effect. Fumble (all dice showing 1): backfire.
- **Aether Attunement:** Characters with Aether 14+ sense Amber impressions and interpret Root-Song fragments.
- **Arborist Relic Attunement:** Requires Aether 12+. Unintuned use: Tier I benefit only regardless of item's actual material tier.

## Design Pillars

1. **Druidic, not industrial.** Shamanic rituals, root-song, sap-reading. Arborists sang wood into shape. Not factories, not machines, not biopunk.
2. **The tree IS the world.** Not a planet with a big tree. The canopy is the sky. The roots are the underworld. There is nothing else.
3. **You are what you wear.** No XP, no leveling. Gear tier is character power. Equipment defines the class.
4. **Death means something.** Permanent death. Characters become Hollows. Emberhome persists. Legacy travels via Heirlooms and Memory Seeds.
5. **The clock is always ticking.** Doom Clock creates urgency. Every action costs the world something.
6. **Metroidvania gating.** Players see locked Vaults early. Come back with tools to open them.
7. **Memory Seeds as legacy.** Per-save recipe unlocks give each campaign a distinct development arc.
8. **NPCs have history.** Persistent memory, cultural bias, faction relationships. The world reacts.
9. **Each season is a complete reality.** Four Groves, four campaigns, four tonal identities — connected by Root-Roads.
