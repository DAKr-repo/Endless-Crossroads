## 1. COSMOLOGY EXPANSION: SIX GROVES

The cosmos has been expanded from four Groves to six. The four Seasonal Groves (Burnwillow, Verdhollow, Solheart, Ashenmere) remain as surface-level world-trees. Two new Groves have been added on a vertical axis:

### The Heartwood — The First Tree (Within)

The Heartwood is not a direction — it is a depth. All four Seasonal Groves are branches of one original tree. The Heartwood is the trunk, accessible not by traveling up or down but by going inward through the wood itself. Bore deep enough through the growth rings of any wall in any dungeon, and you eventually reach the Heartwood — the oldest wood, the First Growth, the seed from which everything grew.

The Heartwood is:
- The Arborists' most sacred space, where the Root-Song was first sung
- A procedural dungeon where rooms are tree anatomy — sap channels as hallways, knots as chambers, resin pockets as vaults
- Each concentric ring is older; the outermost rings show familiar Arborist construction, the inner rings are alien wood so dense it rings like metal
- Enemies are preserved Arborist constructs, amber-locked hostile memories, and the tree's immune responses treating the player as an infection
- Currently under siege — the Choir presses inward, the Silencers' descendants hold the line in silence

**Hidden entrances** are found in:
- Overgrown passages in abandoned dungeon sections
- Behind certain Amber Vaults (some vaults don't contain treasure — they contain inward passages; the golem bows instead of attacking)
- Nooks in branches and Crown zone, grown over by bark
- Discoverable through faction reputation (Allied+), DC 25+ Wits checks, Arborist relics (Dowsing Rod, Memory Seeds), or NPC rumors (Old Cap, Twitch's crystal, Father Oaken's pews)

**The Vault Connection:** The Arborists sealed the Heartwood using the same Amber Vault technology they used for treasure caches. The loot vaults are either dual-purpose or decoys — the real function was to seal the Heartwood. The treasure was bait to keep the technology remembered. The player has been learning to open Heartwood doors without knowing it.

### The Undergrove — Where the Roots Remember (Below)

The shared root network beneath all four Seasonal Groves. Where the Root-Roads go when they go deep enough. A tangle of ancient roots from all four trees, interwoven so densely you cannot tell which belongs to which Grove.

The Undergrove is:
- The compost layer of the universe — where dead things decompose into nutrients for new growth
- Where the Rot is native and natural — decomposition is supposed to happen here
- Contains geological memory of failed Groves that existed before the current Four
- Where Hollows eventually migrate after death
- Where the Choir (see below) directs the Rot from

**The Rot's true nature:** The Rot is not a disease or invasion. It is the Undergrove's decomposition process operating in the wrong place. The boundary between the Undergrove and the living wood was broken by the Choir's song. Decomposition leaked upward.

**Entrances** are in the deepest Zone 4 vaults. The amber on these seals is grey, cracked, visibly stressed. The golems are damaged, corroded. The Rot has been pressing against these doors from the other side for centuries. Opening one gives access but also opens a door that was holding the Rot back.

## 2. THE ARBORISTS — COMPLETE HISTORY

### Who They Were: Resonance Engineers

The Arborists were not builders, smiths, or mages. They were biological musicians — Resonance Engineers who shaped the living world through sound and Aether vibration.

- Their magic was entirely auditory. They sang "Notes of Growth" and "Frequencies of Hardening."
- To make Ironbark, an Arborist sat with a sapling for a year, singing a specific low-frequency hum
- Their cities were "Conducted" — planted as seeds and sung into existence over decades
- Memory Seeds are acoustic recordings pressed into amber — first-person experience, not text
- Communication Crystals (like Twitch's) are Resonance Nodes that vibrate the tree's sap channels to carry voice across Groves

### The Schism: Silencers vs. Choir

When Ashenmere began going still, the Arborists panicked. The seasonal cycle appeared to be slowing. The order split:

**The Silencers** believed the cycle should not be forced. Patience was the answer. The tree knew what it was doing.

**The Choir** believed they could sing a better song — one that would bypass Winter entirely (Autumn directly to Spring), skipping death, skipping the Undergrove's decomposition. Not malice. Grief. They loved the tree too much to accept that part of the cycle involved dying.

### The Collapse (~500 years ago)

The Choir's song disrupted the Autumn-to-Winter transition. The Undergrove's decomposition cycle continued but had nowhere to send its output. Pressure built until the boundary between the root layer and living wood shattered. The Rot leaked upward.

**Ashenmere** was the Choir's first experiment. They tested a "pause Winter" song there before attempting to eliminate it. Instead of pausing, it stopped permanently. Ashenmere is frozen in a feedback loop. The motionless Hollows there are still listening to the original test-song.

### The Great Silencing

The Silencers realized the catastrophe was acoustic. The Root-Song was guiding the Rot upward. The Arborists themselves, saturated with Aether, were the loudest beacons.

They chose:
1. Stop singing entirely, forever
2. Seal the Heartwood using Amber Vaults as acoustic dampeners
3. Seal the Root-Roads to cut transmission paths
4. Withdraw into the Heartwood and enter amber stasis

Knowledge loss over three generations:
- Gen 1 (~500 yrs ago): Knew the songs, forbidden to sing
- Gen 2 (~480 yrs ago): Heard whispered fragments as children
- Gen 3 (~440-400 yrs ago): Found the tools, forgot the music. Emberhome founded.

### The Choir — The Antagonist

The Choir refused to stop singing. They descended into the Undergrove to sing the Rot into submission. They failed — the Rot learned their music.

The Choir became the first intentional Hollows. Not mindless — conducting. They still sing, but the song is the Root-Song played in diminished intervals, in a key that makes living wood rot. They are the reason the Rot has direction and pattern. They believe the four Groves are already dead and they are finishing the cycle.

**Structure:**
- **The Conductor** — the leader, deepest Undergrove, final boss. Root-Song pouring from them in a stream of directed decay.
- **Section Leaders** — scattered through the Undergrove and deep root systems. Each controls Rot in a specific zone. Kill one and the Rot there becomes mindless (less dangerous).
- **The Chorus** — recruited Hollows. Organized, formations, singing. They build fungal structures mimicking Arborist architecture.

### Timescale
- ~500 years ago: The Collapse
- ~480 years ago: Gen 1 dies (knew songs, couldn't sing)
- ~440 years ago: Gen 2 dies (heard fragments)
- ~400 years ago: Gen 3 / Old Cap's generation / Emberhome founded
- Present: 400 years of directed Rot advance

## 3. THE SILENT CULTURE — HEARTWOOD DESCENDANTS

A few hundred Arborist descendants live inside the Heartwood. They have not spoken aloud in 400 years. Their civilization is defined by the absence of song.

### The Painted Songs

The first Silencers encoded the entire Arborist acoustic tradition into visual murals:
- Color = frequency (deep amber for low resonance, pale gold for mid, white for high harmonics)
- Brushstroke direction = phase (wave interaction angles)
- Layering = overtone structure (wet-on-wet pigment bleeding)
- Thickness = amplitude
- Composition = temporal structure (read left to right, the mural plays the song in your mind)

Every child learns to read the murals before they walk. They trace colors with their fingers and hear the songs in their heads — never aloud. They carry the complete Arborist tradition in visual memory. They know how to sing Ironbark into existence. They have never made a sound.

New murals are painted constantly — the culture's newspaper, philosophy, debate forum. Competing arguments are painted as competing murals. The more harmonically elegant composition wins.

### The Gardens

Hand-gardening has replaced wood-singing. The descendants graft, prune, and bend branches over years. A Silent gardener takes ten years to grow what an Arborist sang into existence in an afternoon.

The inhabited sections are breathtaking. Every surface has been touched. Living wood curves in impossible arcs. Flowers grow in patterns that form Painted Song shapes from above — gardens that are also musical scores.

### Communication Hierarchy

**Touch-signs** — tactile sign language pressed into palms and forearms. Daily use. 400 years of evolution has made it as nuanced as spoken language. Jokes, poetry, and arguments all happen through touch.

**The Whisper** — the most sacred form of voice. A whisper is acoustically unique: turbulent airflow without vocal cord vibration. No resonance signature. The tree cannot hear it. The Choir cannot detect it. The descendants discovered this through tragedy — someone spoke, the Rot came; someone whispered, the Rot passed by.

A whisper is never wasted on trivia. To whisper to someone is to trust them with your breath shaped almost into song, held at the very edge of what is safe. Children learn: your voice is not gone. It is the last ember. Guard it.

**The Whisper-Key** now makes acoustic sense: it converts a whisper into vault-opening resonance without adding harmonics. The only safe way to open a vault. The Arborists designed it intentionally — you whisper to the amber.

**Memory Seed notes** — for permanence and distance. Short experiential impressions. Their letters and legal documents.

**Painted Songs** — for knowledge that must outlast the tree itself.

Weight hierarchy: Touch-sign ("pass the water") < Whisper ("I love you" / "someone is dying") < Memory Seed ("this must outlast us") < Painted Song ("this must outlast the tree")

### First Contact

When a surface-dweller arrives speaking in full voice, the descendants react with animal terror. They grab the newcomer, silence them, drag them to a deep chamber. After frantic touch-signs — the eldest leans close. The player feels breath on their ear. A whisper: "You are so loud." The first words spoken to a surface-dweller in 400 years.

### Descendant Factions

**The Roots** (majority) — Conservative. Want to be left alone. Silence has worked for 400 years.

**The Seedlings** (young) — Burn to hear the Painted Songs for real. Want to reach the surface and learn if singing safely is possible. Most likely to help the player. Most likely to be reckless.

**The Grafters** (pragmatists) — The Heartwood is losing ground. Will trade Arborist knowledge for military support against Rot incursions.

## 4. THE CHOIR'S SOUND

The Root-Song on the surface: subsonic hum felt in feet and skull. Warm, rhythmic, like a slow heartbeat.

The Choir's corruption: the same song with intervals wrong. Where the Root-Song resolves (tension into consonance), the Choir's version refuses to resolve. Hangs on diminished intervals. Reaches for relief and lands a half-step flat, every time. Physically: teeth ache, skin crawls, throat tightens.

**Progression by zone:**
- Zone 2: Occasional dissonant undertone. Easy to dismiss.
- Zone 3: Two songs competing. Choir bleeds through in sustained passages.
- Zone 4: Root-Song absent. Choir only. Constant, low, intimate — singing softly into your ear from inside the walls.
- Undergrove: Overpowering. Beautiful like a fever dream. Gear begins to hum sympathetically. Rot treats you as part of the score.

## 5. THE ENDGAME — THREE PATHS

**1. Silence the Choir.** Kill the Conductor and Section Leaders. Rot becomes mindless — still present but no longer directed. The tree stops getting worse but doesn't heal. The Silencers' solution, forever.

**2. Complete the Song.** The cycle needs to complete. Winter cannot be skipped. Fix the Choir's song — add the missing notes. Let the Undergrove finish its work in a controlled way. The tree partially dies and regrows. Ashenmere thaws. Burnwillow loses its fire-leaves. The world changes permanently.

**3. Wake the Sleepers.** Revive the amber-sealed Arborists. They know the original song. Maybe they can sing something new. The hardest path, requiring the most faction support.

## 6. SIX FACTIONS (New — Grounded in Real Willow Ecology)

All factions map to real ecological relationships willow trees have with other organisms.

### The Hive (Bee/Wasp — Pollination)
Collective intelligence. NPCs: The Queen Mother (dying hive-mind), Drone-Captain Vex (speaks in "we"), Stinger (exile with individual thoughts). Services: wax-sealed armor, honey potions, propolis sealant. Exalted reward: Royal Jelly (hive-sense — see through walls 3 rooms). Quest: Queen dying, Rot targeting breeding chambers.

### The Mycelium (Fungal Network — Mycorrhizal)
Wood wide web. NPCs: The Spreading (collective, speaks through mushrooms), Sporecaller Maeth (half-human translator), Old Cap (400-year-old cranky toadstool — was present when Arborists sealed Root-Roads). Services: spore grenades, network fast-travel, decomposition crafting. Exalted reward: Mycelium Map (hidden rooms). Dark side: some patches Rot-corrupted.

### The Dam-Wrights (Beaver-Folk — Hydraulic Mutualism)
Engineers maintaining root-system water flow. NPCs: Foreman Gnaw (gruff, main dam has secret fracture), Kitspool (young dreamer with sap-reversal device), The Old Dam (blind elder, feels vibrations). Services: gear reinforcement, Root-Road shortcuts, siege barricades. Exalted reward: Lodgecraft. Quest: main dam failing, Emberhome floods.

### The Canopy Court (Fae/Moth-Kin — Moth Specialist Symbiosis)
Crown dwellers. Beautiful, alien, cruel like nature. NPCs: Moth Prince (riddles, not real royalty), Silkmother Vray (enchanter, charges in memories), Thorn (clipped-wing exile in Emberhome). Services: silk-weaving (Aether affixes), wind-riding, moth-scale powder. Exalted reward: Mothwing Cloak (invisibility). Dark side: isolationist faction wants to seal Crown.

### The Hag Circle (Decomposers — Bracket Fungi)
Three sisters. Not evil — composting personified. NPCs: Brindlemaw (blind, poisons, secretly immunizing Emberhome water), Kettleblack (jovial cook, prices friendship as leverage), Needlewise (sharp, collects teeth, secretly believes tree can be saved). Services: curse-brewing, Rot processing, fortune-telling (costs a memory). Exalted reward: Blight Tolerance.

### The Heartwood Elders (Ents — Sentient Branches)
ARE the tree. Communicate by growing. NPCs: Ashknot (Rot-burned, thinks at human speed, terrified). Services: living architecture, Heartwood grafting (+1 tier, can't unequip), Amber communion. Exalted reward: Root-Sense (Doom advance warning). Quest: Elder dying (zone collapse), Elder dreaming Rot's dreams.

### Faction Reputation System
Hostile (-2) → Wary (-1) → Neutral (0) → Friendly (+1) → Allied (+2) → Exalted (+3)

Opposing pairs (can't max both):
- Hag Circle vs Heartwood Elders (decomposition vs preservation)
- Canopy Court vs Dam-Wrights (isolation vs infrastructure)
- The Hive vs The Mycelium (surface intel vs underground intel)

### Gear Stat Bonuses (New)
Every equipped item provides exactly +1d6 to its stat pool regardless of tier, plus +tier to the stat SCORE. A Tier I sword = +1d6 and +1 to Might score. A Tier IV sword = +1d6 and +4 to Might score. Same dice, better modifier. To get MORE dice, equip more items. To get better results from those dice, equip higher tier items. Since the modifier formula is `(score - 10) // 2`, every 2 points of gear bonus equals +1 modifier.

## 9. THREAT ECOLOGY — THE THINGS THAT WERE ALWAYS HERE

Threats that existed before the Collapse. The Arborists managed them. Nobody manages them now.

### The Leech Cascade (Aphid Colonies → Ecosystem Death Spiral)

Sap Leeches are dog-sized, pale, translucent colonial organisms. They drain sap from the richest wood. The cascade is a chain reaction:

Leeches drain healthy wood → area weakens → Rot probes the weakness → tree immune response floods healing sap → Gall parasites hijack the immune response → leaked sap attracts more Leeches → spiral accelerates → the Choir notices and lets it run (free territory gain).

**The Hive farms Leeches** for honeydew (key ingredient in honey potions). Their "managed" colonies contribute to the cascade. The Hive is too biologically dependent to admit it.

**Cascade Rooms** appear 1-in-6 in Zone 2+. Four stages from simple Leech colony (Sap-Drained condition) to full structural collapse (Sap-Drained + Spore-Sick + Entangled hazard + Rot creatures + Borer nests).

**Quest tension:** Hive wants colonies protected. Dam-Wrights want them destroyed. Hag Circle offers synthetic honeydew from dead Leeches + Gall tissue — but requires letting the cascade run long enough to harvest.

### The Gall (Parasitic Growth Hijacking)

Tumor-like bulges in walls (1-3 feet across, warm, pulsing). They hijack the tree's immune response — the healthiest wood is most infected. Cutting one releases a Gall Larva (pale, segmented, cat-sized, aggressive) + nutrient-rich sap (ingredient). Contact with ruptured tissue risks Gall-Marked condition.

The Heartwood descendants use managed Galls as a food source — one of their few successful adaptations of Arborist ecosystem management.

### Bark Borers (Structural Tunnelers)

Forearm-length armored beetles that bore geometric gallery networks. Not aggressive to people — they flee if cornered. Their tunnels weaken structure (collapse hazards) and create Rot highways (bypass the sealed bark layer). A Borer-infested room has DC 15 Grit hazard on entry.

Dam-Wrights are their natural enemy. Cleared Borer tunnels can serve as fragile shortcuts between rooms. The Arborists repelled them with specific resonance frequencies — knowledge lost in the Great Silencing.

### Crown Predators (Apex Wildlife)

Storm Raptors, Canopy Stalkers, Emberstorm Phoenixes. Not corrupted — just apex predators. They eat anything that climbs, including Rot creatures, making the Crown the healthiest zone. Inadvertent guardians. The Canopy Court coexists with them carefully. A player without Court guidance is prey.

### The Hollow Tide (Choir Recruitment via Entrainment)

The Choir broadcasts a standing wave. Hollows drift downward into the Undergrove and eventually synchronize — walking in rhythm, then humming, then joining the Chorus. Not mind control — entrainment (pendulum synchronization). Horror: Chorus members wear gear of fallen Seekers the player might recognize — possibly their own previous characters.

### Vault Breach Alert (Acoustic Consequence)

Opening an Amber Vault breaks its acoustic dampener. For the next 3 rooms: 100% enemy spawn, +1 enemy count per room. Vault room itself spawns an immediate wave. Multiple vaults stack. Doom Clock unaffected — this is local Rot response to noise, not global.

## 10. CRIMINAL ELEMENTS — THE ROT WITHIN EMBERHOME

### The Scrappers (Black Market)

Loose network of salvage poachers, amber launderers, gear thieves, and information brokers. Led informally by **Grimjaw** (existing NPC). Not villains — symptoms of scarcity. They fill a market gap the communal system can't. They threaten Emberhome with cynicism, not violence.

### The Hollowborn (Cult of the Inevitable)

Secret faction believing the Rot will win and resistance is hubris. They sabotage Seeker expeditions (misplaced maps, emptied caches), spread small amounts of Blight intentionally, and recruit from the hopeless. Communicate through anonymous Memory Seed messages showing peaceful visions of the tree dying. Led by **The Quiet** — identity unknown.

They might be right — the player's answer to their argument IS the endgame choice. Critically: they don't know the Choir exists. Discovering that the Rot is directed, not natural, would shatter their theology.

### The Ash Runners (Dungeon Bandits)

5-8 rogue former Seekers living in Zone 1, raiding other parties. Led by **Sable** — former squad captain who left after Voss sent her people into Zone 3 without supplies. Code: take your best item, let you live. Can be enemies, allies (best Zone 1 intel), or recruits.

**Critical detail:** Sable has found a Heartwood entrance. She doesn't know what it is. Help her and she shows you. Antagonize her and she sells the location.

## 11. ROOT-ATTUNEMENT — A UNIVERSAL CAPACITY (Reflavored from "gene")

Root-Attunement is NOT a genetic trait, bloodline, or inborn gift. It is a universal capacity — like building muscle or learning language. Everyone in the Groves has it. In most it lies dormant. It awakens through prolonged exposure to Aether, through working with living wood, through years spent in the tree's resonant structures. No one is born "without" it. Some just haven't had the opportunity to develop it.

**The Spectrum of Awakening:**
- **Dormant (most, Aether <12):** Never activated. Can learn Arborist techniques as rote skills. May develop with exposure.
- **Stirring (Aether 12-13):** Awakened by years near Aether sources. Wood "cooperates," gut feelings. Weld's decades at the forge did this. Scout Lira's dungeon time developed her instincts.
- **Resonant (Aether 14-16):** Fully awake. Hear the Root-Song unprompted. Father Oaken's flame responds to them. Could learn to sing if taught.
- **True Arborist (Aether 17+):** Lifelong practice + Aether saturation → physical transformation. NOT a birthright — the result of devotion.
- **Heartwood Descendants:** 400 years of passive immersion. Proof that environment, not birth, drives attunement.

**Key implications:**
- Attunement can deepen during play through Aether exposure, Arborist contact, Heartwood proximity.
- Emberhome's farming traditions work for the Stirring, who attribute it to luck.
- "Wake the Sleepers" endgame requires a Resonant (Aether 14+).
- The Seedlings seek surface Resonants who might hear the Painted Songs.
- Attunement predates all heritage distinctions. Everyone carries it.

## 13. THE FOUR PEOPLES — SHAPED BY SEASON

No traditional fantasy races. The original inhabitants of the First Tree were one people. The four Groves shaped them over millennia through concentrated Aether at four distinct vibrational frequencies. Race is what the tree made your ancestors into.

The Root-Attunement gene functions identically in all four peoples — Aether score determines carrier/resonant status. But the EXPRESSION differs by ancestry.

### Autumn-Born (Burnwillow)
- **Shaped by:** Decay-frequency Aether
- **Physical:** Warm-toned skin (amber, copper, brown). Hair shifts with age — dark → ember-red → ash-white. Resilient to temperature extremes.
- **Aether expression:** Tuned to transition and transformation. Carriers sense when things are about to change. Resonants feel the Doom Clock viscerally.
- **Stat bonus:** +2 Grit. They endure. Root-Song felt as a steady pulse.

### Spring-Born (Verdhollow)
- **Shaped by:** Growth-frequency Aether
- **Physical:** Green-tinged undertones. Tall, rangy. Fast-healing minor cuts. Some have faintly bioluminescent eyes.
- **Aether expression:** Tuned to growth and acceleration. Carriers have real green thumbs. Resonants feel sap pressure and structural soundness.
- **Stat bonus:** +2 Aether. Most naturally gifted at Aether manipulation. The Arborists likely drew disproportionately from this population.

### Summer-Born (Solheart)
- **Shaped by:** Intensity-frequency Aether
- **Physical:** Dark skin, heat/light resistant. Compact, muscular. Eyes tolerate extreme brightness. Calloused from birth.
- **Aether expression:** Tuned to force and intensity. Carriers hit harder through unconscious channeling. Resonants sense energy building toward breaking points.
- **Stat bonus:** +2 Might. They burn. Root-Song felt as heat and pressure.

### Winter-Born (Ashenmere)
- **Shaped by:** Stillness-frequency Aether
- **Physical:** Pale, translucent skin. White/silver hair from birth. Slight build. Slow metabolism. Natural lifespan 120+. Unbothered by cold.
- **Aether expression:** Tuned to perception and stillness. Carriers notice what others miss. Resonants perceive the Root-Song where it's weakest — calibrated to signal over noise. The only people who might detect what's WRONG with the Choir's song.
- **Stat bonus:** +2 Wits. They observe. Root-Song heard as the spaces between notes.

### Emberhome's Population
- Primarily Autumn-Born
- Spring-Born: significant minority (Verdhollow refugees arrived first, growth pushed through seals)
- Summer-Born: rare (brutal journey, valued for forge-work)

## 15. GEAR SETS & AFFIXES — IMPLEMENTED 2026-03-29

### Gear Set System

`set_id` field added to GearItem. 5 sets defined with 2/3/4-piece bonuses. GearGrid detects equipped set pieces and returns active bonuses automatically.

| Set | 2pc | 3pc | 4pc |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Arborist's Legacy | +1 Aether score | Sanctify free action | Auto-crit on Blighted |
| Warden's Watch | +1 DR | Intercept 2 allies | On block: allies +1d6 |
| Rot Hunter's Trophy | Resist Blight | +2 vs Blighted | Kill Blighted heals 1d6 |
| Moonstone Circle | Mending +1d6 | Renewal +2 rounds | Party Regen 1/round |
| Shadowweave | +2 Scout | Backstab double surprise | First attack crits |

20 existing loot items tagged with set_id via LOOT_SET_IDS mapping in content.py.

### Randomized Affix System

`prefix` and `suffix` fields on GearItem. `get_display_name()` produces full names like "Blazing Ironbark Longsword of the Canopy."

**6 Prefixes:** Blazing (+1d4 fire), Frozen (10% slow), Vampiric (heal on kill), Keen (crit 5-6), Volatile (+2 dmg / self-dmg fumble), Rooted (+1 DR stationary)

**5 Suffixes:** ...of the Canopy (Aether pool), ...of Haste (movement), ...of Thorns (reflect), ...of Mending (regen), ...of the Willow (Blight resist)

`roll_affixes(tier, zone_depth)` called automatically on every loot drop. Higher tier and deeper zones = more affixes. Tier I: ~10% chance. Tier IV: ~50% chance of prefix, ~20% chance of both.

### Dice Reminder

Every item gives exactly +1d6 regardless of tier. Tier determines stat score bonus (+1 to +4) and trait scaling. "Blazing Moonstone Helm of Thorns [SET: wardens_watch]" gives +1d6 Wits, +3 to Wits score, Blazing fire damage, Thorns reflect, AND counts toward the Warden's Watch set bonus.
