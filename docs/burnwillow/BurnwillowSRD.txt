BURNWILLOW: System Reference Document
Premise: A procedural dungeon-crawler where equipment defines your class. Goal: Reach the heart of the Burning Willow to stop the Blight. Core Philosophy: You are what you wear. Death is final.

1. The Core Mechanic
To attempt a risky action, the player rolls a pool of six-sided dice (d6) and adds a relevant Stat Modifier.

The Formula:

[Dice Pool] + [Stat Modifier] vs. [Difficulty Class (DC)]

The Cap: A player may never roll more than 5d6 for a single action.

The Base: Characters always start with 1d6 (The "Naked" die). Gear adds additional dice.

Difficulty Classes (DC)
Each step of 3.5 equals one die's average value.
Difficulty DC Examples
Routine 5 Breaking a wooden crate, climbing a knotted rope.
Standard 11 Picking a lock, jumping a chasm, attacking a soldier.
Hard 15 Bending iron bars, deciphering ancient runes.
Elite 22 Resisting dragon fear, outrunning a collapsing tunnel.
Legendary 30+ Swimming up a waterfall, breaking an Arborist seal.
2. Character Creation
Characters are defined by four Core Stats. Roll 4d6 and drop the lowest for each. Assign as desired.

The Four Pillars
MIGHT (Physical): Strength, melee combat, athletics.

WITS (Mental): Perception, ranged accuracy, technology, mechanisms.

GRIT (Fortitude): Health, stamina, poison resistance.

AETHER (Magical): Willpower, spellcasting, attunement.

Stat Modifiers
Score Modifier Score Modifier
8–9 -1 14–15 +2
10–11 0 16–17 +3
12–13 +1 18 +4
Health (HP): 10 + Grit Modifier.

Defense (AC): 10 + Wits Modifier (Modified by Shield/Armor).

3. Equipment & Progression
Characters do not level up. They acquire better gear. The Slots (10 Total): Head, Shoulders, Chest, Arms, Legs, R. Hand, L. Hand, R. Ring, L. Ring, Neck.

Gear Tiers (The Dice Pool)
The material of an item determines how many dice are added to the roll.

Tier 0 (Unarmed): 1d6 Total.

Tier I (Scrap/Wood): Adds +1d6.

Tier II (Iron/Leather): Adds +2d6.

Tier III (Steel/Silver): Adds +3d6.

Tier IV (Mithral/Gold): Adds +4d6.

Armor & Protection
Armor does not make you harder to hit; it reduces damage taken.

Damage Reduction (DR): Subtracted from incoming damage before HP loss.

Example: Chainmail Chest (DR 2). A hit for 5 damage becomes 3 damage.

4. Combat Rules
Combat is played in Rounds.

Initiative (The Ambush): When entering a room with enemies, the party leader rolls Wits vs. Enemy Passive DC.

Success: The Party takes an Ambush Round (Enemies do nothing).

Failure: Roll standard Initiative.

The Turn:

Move: Up to Speed (Standard ~30ft).

Action: Attack, Cast, Skill, or Support.

Universal Actions
Attack: Roll [Weapon Dice] + Stat vs. Enemy Defense.

Dash: Move twice.

Guard: Add +1d6 to Defense rolls until next turn.

Grapple/Shove: Might vs. Might. Success = Prone or Pushed.

Active Support Actions (Gear Dependent)
Intercept (Shield): Take a hit meant for an adjacent ally. Gain +2 DR for that hit.

Command (Banner/Horn): Roll Wits. Success = Ally gains an immediate free Attack or Move.

Bolster (Holy Symbol): Roll Aether. Success = Ally adds +2d6 to their next roll.

Sanctify (Relic): Roll Aether. Create a zone enemies cannot enter.

5. Adventuring Rules
The Lock & Key Economy
Keys: Consumable items. Automatically open 1 Locked Chest/Door.

Lockpicking: Requires Thief Gear (e.g., Skeleton Key Band). Roll Wits vs. Lock DC.

Failure: Lock jams or Trap triggers.

Brute Force: Roll Might.

Success: Opens, but 50% chance to destroy fragile loot inside.

Magic: Implements vs. Scrolls
Implements (Wands/Staves): Infinite use. Scale with Aether. Lower damage than melee weapons, but hit Resistance instead of Defense.

Scrolls: Consumable. High power.

Volatile: When used, roll Aether vs. DC 11 (Standard). Failure = Scroll burns and spell fizzles (or backfires).

Death & The Cycle
Hardcore: When HP reaches 0, the character is dead.

Legacy: The dungeon resets. The town persists (optionally). The player creates a new character (4d6 drop lowest) to brave the Burnwillow again.
